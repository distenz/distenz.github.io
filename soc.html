Separation of Concerns

Original essey of Dijkstra

"Modularization", a reference to the way in which the knowledge in the world has been arranged, is probably the best concise answer.

For the separation to be meaningful, we have also an internal and an external requirement.

External requirement: "thin interface"

the more self-supporting such an intellectual subuniverse, the less detailed the knowledge that its practitioners need about other areas of human endeavour, the greater its viability.

  • The purpose of the domain of knowledge is to provide useful abilities
  • It takes time and energy to know a domain
  • Therefore there is a cost to the abilities
  • The thin interface is the minimum input information to a given domain to be able to gain a or a desired set of abilities from said domain.

Internal requirement: "coherence of state and behavior"

The internal requirement is one of coherence: the knowledge must support the abilities and the abilities must enable us to improve the knowledge.

  • I understand

    • coherence as literary "being connected to"
    • knowledge as state
    • abilities as behavior
  • Behavior references state as reading or writing it
  • Behavior is connected to state or other behavior by shared input/output shapes
  • If a shape is from, or for the module interface
